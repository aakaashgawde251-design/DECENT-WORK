Empowering Individuals Through Decent Work

Introduction to Decent Work

Decent Work, a cornerstone concept from the International Labour Organization (ILO), defines the conditions under which people can work in freedom, equity, security, and human dignity. It ensures productive work that provides fair income, workplace security, social protection, and equal opportunities for all.

"Work is not just earning; it is dignity and empowerment."

SDG 8: Decent Work & Economic Growth

Sustainable Development Goal 8 calls for promotion of sustained, inclusive, and sustainable economic growth, along with full and productive employment and decent work for all by 2030. Key focus areas include eradicating forced labour, promoting safe workplaces, and ensuring equal pay.

Skill Development (Education & Training)

Investment in quality education and vocational training ensures workers acquire in-demand skills for the evolving global economy. This leads to better career prospects and stability.

Job Opportunities (Full & Productive Employment)

Decent work requires robust policies that stimulate economic activity, support entrepreneurship, and create meaningful job openings for vulnerable groups.

Fair Wages (Equitable Pay)

Awareness and enforcement of fair wages ensure compensation is enough for a dignified life and that workers receive equal pay for work of equal value.

Fair Wages & Worker Rights

Understanding Fair Wages

Fair wages ensure workers are respected, paid legally, and treated equally. It prevents exploitation and supports sustainable development.

  • Achieving higher levels of economic productivity

Your Worker Rights

Every worker has the right to:

  • Fair Wages: At least minimum wage + living wage
  • Safe Workplace: Protective equipment and safe conditions
  • Maximum Work Hours: 8 hours per day, 48 hours per week
  • Regular Leave: Paid leave and holidays
  • Social Security: Health insurance and pension
  • Freedom of Association: Right to join unions
  • No Discrimination: Equal pay for equal work
